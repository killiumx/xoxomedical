🚑 EMS Patient Interview Guide

Step-by-step questions to ask — fill the form as you go

💡 Tip: Keep this guide open alongside the form. Work through each section in order — ask the question, then fill in the answer before moving on.
1

Patient Identity

💬
"Can I get your full name?"
First and last name. If unconscious, check ID or ask bystanders.
→ Full Name field
💬
"What's your date of birth?"
Format: DD/MM/YYYY
→ Date of Birth field
💬
"Do you have a Citizen ID on you?"
Check wallet if patient is incapacitated.
→ Citizen ID field
💬
"Who should we contact in an emergency? Name and number?"
→ Emergency Contact field
2

Chief Complaint

💬
"What brings you in today? What happened?"
Let them describe freely. Note time of onset.
→ Reason for Visit / Exam Type
💬
"On a scale of 0 to 10 — zero being no pain at all, ten being the absolute worst — where are you right now?"
0–3 mild, 4–6 moderate, 7–10 severe/critical.
→ Pain Level (Vitals)
3

Vitals — Measure & Record

📟
Take Heart Rate reading
Normal: 60–100 bpm. Below 60 = bradycardia. Above 100 = tachycardia.
→ Heart Rate dropdown
📟
Take Blood Pressure reading
Normal: 90–130 systolic / 60–85 diastolic. Note both numbers.
→ Blood Pressure dropdown
📟
Check O2 Saturation via pulse oximeter
Normal: 96–100%. Below 94% = concerning. Below 90% = critical.
→ O2 Saturation dropdown
📟
Take Temperature
Normal: 97–99°F. Above 100.4°F = fever. Above 103°F = critical.
→ Temperature dropdown
📟
Count Respiratory Rate (breaths per minute)
Normal: 12–20 br/min. Count for 30 seconds, multiply by 2.
→ Resp. Rate dropdown
📟
Assess Level of Consciousness
Is patient alert and talking clearly? Confused? Responding to voice only? Not responding?
→ Consciousness dropdown
4

Medical History

💬
"Any known allergies — medications, food, anything?"
If none, write "NKDA" (No Known Drug Allergies).
→ Known Allergies field (Patient Info)
💬
"Are you currently taking any medications?"
Ask for name, dose, and how often. Include OTC meds and supplements.
→ Current Medications (Physical Exam)
💬
"Do you have any existing medical conditions we should know about?"
Diabetes, heart disease, asthma, blood disorders, etc.
→ Doctor's Notes / Examination Notes
5

Physical Examination — General Check-Up

🩺
Assess Head & Neurological
"Any headache, dizziness, or confusion?" Check for visible injuries, pupils equal and reactive?
→ Head / Neuro + Eyes dropdowns
🩺
Auscultate Chest & Heart
"Any chest pain or difficulty breathing?" Listen for abnormal sounds, check rhythm.
→ Chest / Respiratory + Cardiovascular dropdowns
🩺
Palpate Abdomen
"Any abdominal pain or tenderness?" Check for rigidity, guarding, distension.
→ Abdomen dropdown
🩺
Check Extremities & Skin
Full range of motion? Any swelling, bruising, lacerations, fractures? Color and temperature of skin?
→ Extremities + Skin dropdowns
6

Pregnancy — Additional Questions

💬
"How far along are you?"
Weeks of pregnancy / trimester. If unknown, note "patient unsure."
→ Gestational Age dropdown
💬
"Is this your first pregnancy?"
Note any previous pregnancies, C-sections, or miscarriages.
→ Previous Pregnancies dropdown
💬
"Are you feeling baby movement?" / "Any contractions, bleeding, or pain?"
Reduced movement or bleeding = escalate immediately. Check complications dropdown.
→ Complications / Flags dropdown
📟
Check Fetal Heart Rate with Doppler if available
Normal FHR: 110–170 bpm. Outside this range = monitor closely.
→ Fetal Heart Rate dropdown
7

Wrap Up & Handover

📝
Complete Diagnosis & Plan section
Enter primary findings, any meds prescribed, and follow-up instructions. Hit ✨ Auto-Fill Notes to generate a draft clinical note.
→ Diagnosis & Plan section
📝
Set Patient Status & sign off
Select Stable / Observation / Critical / Discharged. Enter your name and badge number in Attending Staff.
→ Patient Status + Attending Staff
📤
Export & Send Report
Use Export Report to preview the full PDF, or paste your Discord webhook URL and hit Send Report as Image.
